October 1st 2021 to November 1st 2020

The Defold Foundation and Yandex.Games is hereby inviting Defold developers across the world to join a month long game jam. Developers will be challenged to create a themed web game showing their creative and developer skills as well as a good integration with the Yandex.Games SDK. The proud winner and runner-ups will receive marketing and advertisment for their submitted game, hardware, swag and bragging rights!




Yandex.Games

Yandex.Games is a catalog of browser-based online games that can be played on smartphones or desktop devices and require no installation. Most games are also available offline (code for these games is added to the device cache during the first gaming session).

Rules

  • You must create an original game for the game jam and you must develop it during the game jam.
  • You are allowed to use existing tools, frameworks and libraries when creating the game.
  • You must use the Defold game engine when you create your game.
  • You must incorporate some aspect of the theme in the game.
  • You must integrate and use the Yandex.Games SDK in the game.
  • You can create the game on your own or as part of a team.

Judging

The submitted games will be judged and ranked in three categories:

  • Player's Choice - The games will be ranked based on the average star rating given by the players
  • Yandex' Choice - The team at Yandex will rank the games based on how well they believe the games will perform on the Yandex platform
  • Defold's Choice - The Defold Foundation will rank the games based on technical excellence

The winners will be announced two weeks after the game jam has ended. The announcement will be made here, on the Defold blog and on Twitter.

Submissions

Submissions to this jam must be made through the Yandex.Games dashboard. The game must pass Yandex moderation before it is accepted into the competition. You have two weeks from the end of the game jam to submit updates to the Yandex.Games SDK integration to pass moderation. You are not allowed to make any significant changes to the game itself during this period.
